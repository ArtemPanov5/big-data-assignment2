Abra Honda is a barrio in the municipality of Camuy, Puerto Rico. Its population in 2010 was 1,943. ==History== Puerto Rico was ceded by Spain in the aftermath of the Spanish–American War under the terms of the Treaty of Paris of 1898 and became an unincorporated territory of the United States. In 1899, the United States Department of War conducted a census of Puerto Rico finding that the population of Abra Honda barrio was 1,420. ==Ernesto Memorial Chapel== The Ernesto Memorial Chapel, a historic church, made of stone, is located in Abra Honda barrio. ==Gallery== Abrahonda.jpg|Ernesto Memorial Chapel ==See also== * List of communities in Puerto Rico == References == ==External links== * Category:Barrios of Camuy, Puerto Rico
