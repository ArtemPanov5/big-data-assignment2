Adolphe Anne François Choquart (Toulouse, 12 February 1800 – after 1859) was a 19th-century French writer, librettist and playwright. A cavalry captain then a bodyguard, his plays were presented on the Parisian stages of the 19th century, including the Théâtre du Vaudeville, the Théâtre de l'Opéra-Comique and the Théâtre des Nouveautés. Alexandre Dumas said of him that he was the last of the musketeers.Le Mousquetaire n°59 18 January 1854. == Works == *1827: M.
